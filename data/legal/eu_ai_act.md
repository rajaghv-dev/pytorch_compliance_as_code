# EU Artificial Intelligence Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)

Official Journal of the European Union, L series, No. 2024/1689, dated 12 July 2024

## Preamble

**THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT AND THE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,**

Having regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union, and in particular Articles 16 and 114 thereof,

Having regard to the proposal from the European Commission,

After transmission of the draft legislative act to the national parliaments,

Having regard to the opinion of the European Economic and Social Committee,

Having regard to the opinion of the European Central Bank,

Having regard to the opinion of the Committee of the Regions,

Acting in accordance with the ordinary legislative procedure,

**Whereas:**

1. The purpose of this Regulation is to improve the functioning of the internal market by laying down a uniform legal framework in particular for the development, the placing on the market, the putting into service and the use of artificial intelligence systems (AI systems) in the Union, in accordance with Union values, to promote the uptake of human centric and trustworthy artificial intelligence (AI) while ensuring a high level of protection of health, safety, fundamental rights as enshrined in the Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union (the 'Charter'), including democracy, the rule of law and environmental protection, to protect against the harmful effects of AI systems in the Union, and to support innovation.

2. This Regulation should be applied in accordance with the values of the Union enshrined as in the Charter, facilitating the protection of natural persons, undertakings, democracy, the rule of law and environmental protection, while boosting innovation and employment and making the Union a leader in the uptake of trustworthy AI.

3. AI systems can be easily deployed in a large variety of sectors of the economy and many parts of society, including across borders, and can easily circulate throughout the Union. Certain Member States have already explored the adoption of national rules to ensure that AI is trustworthy and safe and is developed and used in accordance with fundamental rights obligations.

4. AI is a fast evolving family of technologies that contributes to a wide array of economic, environmental and societal benefits across the entire spectrum of industries and social activities.

5. At the same time, depending on the circumstances regarding its specific application, use, and level of technological development, AI may generate risks and cause harm to public interests and fundamental rights that are protected by Union law.

6. Given the major impact that AI can have on society and the need to build trust, it is vital for AI and its regulatory framework to be developed in accordance with Union values.

7. In order to ensure a consistent and high level of protection of public interests as regards health, safety and fundamental rights, common rules for high-risk AI systems should be established.

8. A Union legal framework laying down harmonised rules on AI is therefore needed to foster the development, use and uptake of AI in the internal market.

9. Harmonised rules applicable to the placing on the market, the putting into service and the use of high-risk AI systems should be laid down consistently with Regulation (EC) No 765/2008, Decision No 768/2008/EC and Regulation (EU) 2019/1020.

10. The fundamental right to the protection of personal data is safeguarded in particular by Regulations (EU) 2016/679 and (EU) 2018/1725 and Directive (EU) 2016/680.

11. This Regulation should be without prejudice to the provisions regarding the liability of providers of intermediary services as set out in Regulation (EU) 2022/2065.

12. The notion of 'AI system' in this Regulation should be clearly defined and should be closely aligned with the work of international organisations working on AI.

13. The notion of 'deployer' referred to in this Regulation should be interpreted as any natural or legal person, including a public authority, agency or other body, using an AI system under its authority.

14. The notion of 'biometric data' used in this Regulation should be interpreted in light of the notion of biometric data as defined in Article 4, point (14) of Regulation (EU) 2016/679.

15. The notion of 'biometric identification' referred to in this Regulation should be defined as automated recognition of physical, physiological and behavioural human features.

16. The notion of 'biometric categorisation' referred to in this Regulation should be defined as assigning natural persons to specific categories on the basis of their biometric data.

17. The notion of 'remote biometric identification system' referred to in this Regulation should be defined functionally.

18. The notion of 'emotion recognition system' referred to in this Regulation should be defined as an AI system for the purpose of identifying or inferring emotions or intentions of natural persons.

19. For the purposes of this Regulation the notion of 'publicly accessible space' should be understood as referring to any physical space that is accessible to an undetermined number of natural persons.

20. In order to obtain the greatest benefits from AI systems while protecting fundamental rights, health and safety and to enable democratic control, AI literacy should equip providers, deployers and affected persons with the necessary notions to make informed decisions regarding AI systems.

21. In order to ensure a level playing field and an effective protection of rights and freedoms of individuals across the Union, the rules established by this Regulation should apply to providers of AI systems in a non-discriminatory manner.

22. In light of their digital nature, certain AI systems should fall within the scope of this Regulation even when they are not placed on the market, put into service, or used in the Union.

23. This Regulation should also apply to Union institutions, bodies, offices and agencies when acting as a provider or deployer of an AI system.

24. This Regulation is without prejudice to the competence of the Union and the Member States to support research and innovation activities.

---

## Article 1 - Subject-matter

This Regulation lays down:
(a) harmonised rules for the placing on the market, the putting into service and the use of AI systems;
(b) harmonised rules for the placing on the market, the putting into service and the use of high-risk AI systems;
(c) harmonised rules for the placing on the market of general-purpose AI models.

---

## Article 2 - Scope

1. This Regulation shall apply to:
   (a) providers placing on the market or putting into service AI systems in the Union, regardless of whether those providers are established in the Union or in a third country;
   (b) deployers of AI systems established in the Union;
   (c) providers and deployers of AI systems established in a third country where the output produced by the AI system is intended to be used in the Union.

2. This Regulation shall not apply to AI systems developed or used exclusively for military, defence or national security purposes.

3. This Regulation shall not apply to public authorities in a third country and international organisations when acting in the framework of cooperation or international agreements concluded at Union or national level.

---

## Article 3 - Definitions

For the purposes of this Regulation:

(a) 'AI system' means a machine-based system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy, that may exhibit adaptiveness after deployment, and that, for explicit or implicit objectives, infers from inputs how to generate outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions that can influence physical or virtual environments;

(b) 'provider' means a natural or legal person, including a public authority, that develops an AI system, whether for placement on the market or for own use, with a view to placing it on the market or putting it into service under its own name or trademark;

(c) 'deployer' means a natural or legal person, including a public authority, that uses an AI system under its authority, except where the AI system is used in the course of a personal non-professional activity;

(d) 'distributor' means a natural or legal person in the supply chain, other than the provider or the deployer, that makes an AI system available on the market;

(e) 'importer' means a natural or legal person who places on the market an AI system that bears the name or trademark of a natural or legal person established in the Union;

(f) 'place on the market' means the making available of an AI system on the Union market;

(g) 'putting into service' means the making available of an AI system for the first time for direct or indirect use in the Union;

(h) 'high-risk AI system' means an AI system listed in Annex III.

---

## Article 4 - Annexes

The Annexes to this Regulation form an integral part thereof.

---

## Article 5 - Prohibited practices

1. The following AI practices shall be prohibited:
   (a) the placing on the market, putting into service, or use of an AI system that deploys subliminal techniques beyond a person's consciousness to materially distort a person's behaviour;
   (b) the placing on the market, putting into service, or use of an AI system that exploits any weakness of a person or group to materially distort a person's behaviour;
   (c) the placing on the market, putting into service, or use of AI systems for social scoring by public authorities or on their behalf;
   (d) the use of real-time remote biometric identification systems in publicly accessible spaces for law enforcement purposes.

2. The prohibition in paragraph 1, point (d), shall be subject to the exceptions and requirements laid down in Article 6.

---

## Article 6 - Exceptions to prohibitions

1. By way of derogation from Article 5(1), point (d), the use of real-time remote biometric identification systems in publicly accessible spaces for law enforcement purposes may be authorised under the conditions set out in this Article.

2. The use shall be authorized only for the purpose of:
   (a) the targeted search for specific potential victims of a crime;
   (b) the prevention of a specific, substantial and imminent threat to the life or physical safety of natural persons;
   (c) the targeted search for persons suspected of having committed a serious crime.

---

## Article 7 - Penalties

1. Member States shall lay down the rules on penalties applicable to infringements of this Regulation.

2. Member States shall provide for administrative fines of up to EUR 35 000 000 or, in case of undertakings, up to 6 % of their total worldwide annual turnover for the preceding financial year, whichever is higher.

---

## TITLE II - HIGH-RISK AI SYSTEMS

---

## Article 8 - Classification

1. Without prejudice to paragraph 3, all AI systems that are intended to be used as safety components of products or are themselves products shall be considered high-risk.

2. High-risk AI systems listed in Annex III shall be those where the AI system is intended to be used as a safety component of a product, or is itself a product, covered by the Union harmonisation legislation listed in Annex II.

---

## Article 9 - Requirements for high-risk AI systems

High-risk AI systems shall meet the following requirements:

(a) risk management system;
(b) data governance and data quality;
(c) technical documentation;
(d) record-keeping;
(e) transparency;
(f) human oversight;
(g) accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity.

---

## Article 10 - Risk management system

1. A risk management system shall be established, implemented, documented and maintained in relation to high-risk AI systems.

2. The risk management system shall be understood as a continuous iterative process running throughout the entire lifecycle of a high-risk AI system.

---

## Article 11 - Data governance and data quality

1. Providers of high-risk AI systems shall put in place data governance and management practices.

2. Training, validation and testing data sets shall be relevant, representative, free of errors and complete.

---

## Article 12 - Technical documentation

1. The technical documentation of a high-risk AI system shall be drawn up before that system is placed on the market or put into service.

2. The technical documentation shall contain the information necessary to assess the AI system's compliance with the relevant requirements.

---

## Article 13 - Record-keeping

1. High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed with capabilities to automatically record events.

2. The capabilities shall logging of the AI system's operational life cycle.

---

## Article 14 - Transparency

1. High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way that their operation is sufficiently transparent to enable deployers to interpret the system output.

2. The provider shall ensure that the high-risk AI system is accompanied by instructions for use.

---

## Article 15 - Human oversight

1. High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed so that they can be effectively overseen by natural persons.

2. Human oversight shall aim at preventing or minimising risks.

---

## Article 16 - Accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity

1. High-risk AI systems shall achieve an appropriate level of accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity.

2. The level of accuracy and the relevant metrics shall be declared in the instructions accompanying the system.

---

## Article 17 - Quality management system

1. Providers of high-risk AI systems shall put in place a quality management system.

2. The quality management system shall be documented in a systematic and orderly manner.

---

## Article 18 - Post-market monitoring

1. Providers of high-risk AI systems shall put in place a post-market monitoring system.

2. The post-market monitoring system shall actively and systematically collect, document and analyse relevant data.

---

## Article 19 - Reporting obligations

1. Providers of high-risk AI systems shall report to the relevant authorities of Member States.

2. The reporting shall include any serious incident or malfunction that constitutes a breach of Union law.

---

## Article 20 - Logging obligations

1. Providers of high-risk AI systems shall ensure that the AI system is capable of automatically logging events.

2. Logging capabilities shall ensure traceability of the AI system's outputs.

---

## TITLE III - OBLIGATIONS OF PROVIDERS AND DEPLOYERS

---

## Article 21 - Obligations of providers

Providers of AI systems shall:

(a) ensure that the AI system complies with the requirements in Title II;
(b) draw up the technical documentation;
(c) ensure that the appropriate conformity assessment procedure is carried out;
(d) affix the CE marking;
(e) draw up an EU declaration of conformity;
(f) ensure that the AI system is accompanied by the required information.

---

## Article 22 - Obligations of deployers

1. Deployers of high-risk AI systems shall use such systems in accordance with the instructions for use.

2. Deployers shall assign natural persons to ensure human oversight.

3. Deployers shall monitor the operation of the high-risk AI system.

---

## Article 23 - Obligations of distributors

Distributors shall verify that the AI system bears the required CE marking.

---

## Article 24 - Obligations of importers

Importers shall verify that the AI system is accompanied by the required documentation.

---

## TITLE IV - TRANSPARENCY OBLIGATIONS FOR CERTAIN AI SYSTEMS

---

## Article 50 - Transparency obligations

1. Providers shall ensure that AI systems intended to interact with natural persons are designed and developed in such a way that the person is informed that they are interacting with an AI system.

2. Providers of AI systems that generate audio, image, video or text content shall ensure that the outputs are marked in a machine-readable format.

---

## TITLE V - MEASURES IN SUPPORT OF INNOVATION

---

## Article 56 - Regulatory sandboxes

1. Member States shall ensure that national competent authorities establish AI regulatory sandboxes.

2. The AI regulatory sandboxes shall enable providers to place on the market or put into service AI systems under terms and conditions.

---

## TITLE VI - GOVERNANCE

---

## Article 60 - European Artificial Intelligence Board

1. A European Artificial Intelligence Board (the Board) is hereby established.

2. The Board shall be composed of representatives of Member States.

---

## TITLE VII - IMPLEMENTATION AND ENFORCEMENT

---

## Article 65 - Market surveillance

Member States shall ensure that their market surveillance authorities exercise the powers assigned to them in accordance with this Regulation.

---

## Article 71 - Complaints

1. Any natural or legal person may lodge a complaint with the relevant market surveillance authority.

2. The complaint shall contain a description of the relevant facts.
