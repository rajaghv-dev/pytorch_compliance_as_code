# CHAPTER II - PROHIBITED PRACTICES

## Article 5 - Prohibited practices

1. The following AI practices shall be prohibited:

   (a) the placing on the market, putting into service, or use of an AI system that deploys subliminal techniques beyond a person's consciousness to materially distort a person's behaviour in a manner that causes or is likely to cause that person significant harm;

   (b) the placing on the market, putting into service, or use of an AI system that exploits any weakness of a natural person or group of persons due to their known or predictable psychological or physical vulnerability or situation to materially distort that person or persons' behaviour in a manner that causes or is likely to cause that person or persons significant harm;

   (c) the placing on the market, putting into service, or use of AI systems by public authorities or on their behalf for the evaluation of the eligibility of natural persons for public assistance benefits and services, or for making decisions based solely on automated processing which produce legal effects concerning them;

   (d) the use of real-time remote biometric identification systems in publicly accessible spaces for law enforcement purposes.

2. The prohibition in paragraph 1, point (d), shall be subject to the exceptions and requirements laid down in Article 6.

---

## Article 6 - Exceptions to prohibitions for remote biometric identification

1. By way of derogation from Article 5(1), point (d), the use of real-time remote biometric identification systems in publicly accessible spaces for law enforcement purposes may be authorised only for the following purposes:

   (a) the targeted search for specific potential victims of a crime, including missing persons;

   (b) the prevention of a specific, substantial and imminent threat to the life or physical safety of natural persons;

   (c) the targeted search for persons suspected of having committed a serious crime.

2. The use shall be authorised only if all of the following conditions are met:

   (a) the use is strictly necessary for the achievement of the purpose;

   (b) there is no less intrusive alternative available;

   (c) the use is subject to prior judicial or administrative authorisation;

   (d) the use is limited in time and geographic scope.

---

## Article 7 - Penalties

1. Member States shall lay down the rules on penalties applicable to infringements of this Regulation.

2. Member States shall provide for administrative fines of up to EUR 35 000 000 or, in case of undertakings, up to 6 % of their total worldwide annual turnover for the preceding financial year, whichever is higher.

3. By way of derogation from paragraph 2, Member States shall provide for:

   (a) in case of non-compliance with the obligations under this Regulation, fines of up to EUR 7 500 000 or, for undertakings, up to 1 % of their total worldwide annual turnover for the preceding financial year;

   (b) in case of supply of incorrect, incomplete or misleading information to notified bodies and national competent authorities, fines of up to EUR 7 500 000.

4. The setting of administrative fines shall take into account the nature, gravity and duration of the infringement, the degree of responsibility, and the intentional or negligent character of the infringement.
