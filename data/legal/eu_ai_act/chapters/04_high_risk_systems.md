# CHAPTER III - HIGH-RISK AI SYSTEMS

## Article 8 - Classification

1. Without prejudice to paragraph 3, all AI systems that are intended to be used as safety components of products or are themselves products shall be considered high-risk.

2. AI systems listed in Annex III shall be considered high-risk where they fall within the scope of the areas specified in that Annex.

3. By way of derogation from paragraphs 1 and 2, an AI system shall not be considered high-risk if it does not pose a risk of harm to the health, safety or fundamental rights of natural persons.

---

## Article 9 - Requirements for high-risk AI systems

High-risk AI systems shall meet the following requirements:

(a) **Risk management system**: A risk management system shall be established, implemented, documented and maintained in relation to high-risk AI systems.

(b) **Data governance and data quality**: Providers of high-risk AI systems shall put in place data governance and management practices. Training, validation and testing data sets shall be relevant, representative, free of errors and complete.

(c) **Technical documentation**: The technical documentation shall be drawn up before the system is placed on the market or put into service and shall contain the information necessary to assess the AI system's compliance with the relevant requirements.

(d) **Record-keeping**: High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed with capabilities to automatically record events. The logging capabilities shall ensure traceability of the AI system's functioning throughout its lifecycle.

(e) **Transparency**: High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way that their operation is sufficiently transparent to enable deployers to interpret the system output. The provider shall ensure that the high-risk AI system is accompanied by instructions for use.

(f) **Human oversight**: High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed so that they can be effectively overseen by natural persons during the period of use.

(g) **Accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity**: High-risk AI systems shall achieve an appropriate level of accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity.

---

## Article 10 - Risk management system

1. A risk management system shall be established, implemented, documented and maintained in relation to high-risk AI systems.

2. The risk management system shall be understood as a continuous iterative process running throughout the entire lifecycle of a high-risk AI system.

3. The risk management system shall include:

   (a) identification and analysis of known and foreseeable risks;

   (b) evaluation of the risks;

   (c) adoption of risk mitigation measures;

   (d) monitoring and review of risks.

---

## Article 11 - Data governance and data quality

1. Providers of high-risk AI systems shall put in place data governance and management practices.

2. Practices shall cover:

   (a) the relevant design choices for data processing;

   (b) data collection;

   (c) data processing;

   (d) data preparation;

   (e) data quality assurance.

3. Training, validation and testing data sets shall be relevant, representative, free of errors and complete.

4. Data sets shall have appropriate statistical properties.

---

## Article 12 - Technical documentation

1. The technical documentation of a high-risk AI system shall be drawn up before that system is placed on the market or put into service and shall be kept up-to-date.

2. The technical documentation shall contain the information necessary to assess the AI system's compliance with the relevant requirements.

3. The documentation shall be written in a clear and comprehensible manner.

---

## Article 13 - Record-keeping

1. High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed with capabilities to automatically record events.

2. The logging capabilities shall:

   (a) enable recording of the period of use;

   (b) enable recording of the input data;

   (c) enable recording of the output data;

   (d) enable identification of natural persons involved in the verification of results.

---

## Article 14 - Transparency

1. High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way that their operation is sufficiently transparent to enable deployers to interpret the system output.

2. The provider shall ensure that the high-risk AI system is accompanied by instructions for use.

3. Instructions shall contain:

   (a) identity and contact details of the provider;

   (b) the purpose of the AI system;

   (c) the known or foreseeable circumstances leading to risks;

   (d) the expected lifetime of the system.

---

## Article 15 - Human oversight

1. High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed so that they can be effectively overseen by natural persons.

2. Human oversight shall aim at preventing or minimising risks that may arise when an AI system is used.

3. Human oversight measures shall include:

   (a) the ability to understand the AI system's capabilities;

   (b) the ability to monitor the AI system's operation;

   (c) the ability to intervene in the operation or pause the system.

---

## Article 16 - Accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity

1. High-risk AI systems shall achieve an appropriate level of accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity.

2. The level of accuracy and the relevant metrics shall be declared in the instructions accompanying the system.

3. The AI system shall be resilient to errors, faults or inconsistencies.

---

## Article 17 - Quality management system

1. Providers of high-risk AI systems shall put in place a quality management system.

2. The quality management system shall be documented in a systematic and orderly manner.

3. It shall ensure:

   (a) compliance with this Regulation;

   (b) the application of quality objectives;

   (c) the planning, implementation and control of processes.

---

## Article 18 - Post-market monitoring

1. Providers of high-risk AI systems shall put in place a post-market monitoring system.

2. The post-market monitoring system shall actively and systematically collect, document and analyse relevant data.

3. It shall enable the evaluation of AI system performance and compliance with requirements.

---

## Article 19 - Reporting obligations

1. Providers of high-risk AI systems shall report to the relevant authorities of Member States:

   (a) any serious incident or malfunction that constitutes a breach of Union law;

   (b) any cases of non-compliance with the prohibitions in Article 5.

2. The reporting shall be made without undue delay and in any event within two days.

---

## Article 20 - Logging obligations

1. Providers of high-risk AI systems shall ensure that the AI system is capable of automatically logging the events referred to in Article 13.

2. Logs shall be kept for a period of ten years.

3. Logs shall be made available to national competent authorities upon request.
