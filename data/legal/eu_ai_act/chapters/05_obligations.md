# CHAPTER IV - OBLIGATIONS OF PROVIDERS AND DEPLOYERS

## Article 21 - Obligations of providers

Providers of AI systems shall:

(a) ensure that their AI systems comply with the requirements set out in Chapter III;

(b) draw up the technical documentation as required by Article 12;

(c) ensure that the appropriate conformity assessment procedure is carried out;

(d) affix the CE marking to indicate conformity with this Regulation;

(e) draw up an EU declaration of conformity as required by this Regulation;

(f) ensure that the AI system is accompanied by the information required by Article 14;

(g) put in place a quality management system as required by Article 17;

(h) put in place a post-market monitoring system as required by Article 18;

(i) take the necessary corrective actions when non-compliance is detected;

(j) inform distributors and importers of any non-compliance;

(k) upon request, provide all information to national competent authorities.

---

## Article 22 - Obligations of deployers

1. Deployers of high-risk AI systems shall use such systems in accordance with the instructions for use.

2. Deployers shall assign natural persons to ensure human oversight.

3. Deployers shall monitor the operation of the high-risk AI system.

4. Where a deployer has reasons to consider that the use of a high-risk AI system may pose a risk to persons, it shall inform the provider and suspend the use of the system.

5. Deployers shall keep logs of the use of high-risk AI systems.

6. Deployers shall cooperate with the relevant authorities in any action they take.

---

## Article 23 - Obligations of distributors

1. Before making an AI system available on the market, distributors shall verify that:

   (a) the AI system bears the CE marking;

   (b) the AI system is accompanied by the required documentation;

   (c) the provider has fulfilled the requirements under this Regulation.

2. Where a distributor has reasons to consider that an AI system does not comply with this Regulation, it shall not make it available on the market.

3. Distributors shall cooperate with the relevant authorities.

---

## Article 24 - Obligations of importers

1. Before placing an AI system on the market, importers shall verify that:

   (a) the AI system complies with this Regulation;

   (b) the required documentation is available;

   (c) the CE marking is affixed.

2. Importers shall indicate their name, registered trade name or registered trademark on the AI system.

3. Importers shall ensure that the provider has drawn up the technical documentation.

---

## Article 25 - Obligations of users of AI systems

Users of AI systems that are not high-risk shall:

(a) use AI systems in accordance with the instructions for use;

(b) not make alterations to the AI system that may affect its compliance;

(c) keep logs of the use of AI systems where such logging is under their control.

---

## Article 26 - Obligations of manufacturers

When an AI system is integrated into a product that is placed on the market as a single product, the manufacturer of the product shall comply with this Regulation.

---

## Article 27 - EU declaration of conformity

1. The EU declaration of conformity shall contain:

   (a) name and address of the provider;

   (b) description of the AI system;

   (c) declaration that the AI system complies with this Regulation;

   (d) list of harmonised standards applied;

   (e) place and date of the declaration;

   (f) identity of the person who signed.

2. The declaration shall be kept for ten years.

---

## Article 28 - CE marking

1. The CE marking shall consist of the initials "CE".

2. The CE marking shall be affixed visibly, legibly and indelibly to the AI system or its data plate.

3. Where it is not possible to affix the CE marking on the AI system, it shall be affixed to the packaging or accompanying documents.

4. Member States shall ensure proper implementation of the CE marking system.
