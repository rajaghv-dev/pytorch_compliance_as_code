# GDPR - CHAPTER 4 - CONTROLLER AND PROCESSOR

## Article 24 - Responsibility of the controller

The controller shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure processing in accordance with this Regulation.

The controller shall demonstrate compliance with the principles.

---

## Article 25 - Data protection by design and by default

The controller shall implement data protection principles by design and by default.

This includes:

(a) pseudonymisation;

(b) minimization of data collected;

(c) transparency;

(d) the ability to restore access.

---

## Article 26 - Joint controllers

Where two or more controllers jointly determine the purposes and means of processing, they shall be joint controllers.

They shall determine their respective responsibilities by agreement.

---

## Article 27 - Representative of controllers not established in the Union

Controllers not established in the Union shall designate a representative in the Union.

---

## Article 28 - Processor

The processor shall only process on the basis of documented instructions from the controller.

The processor shall ensure confidentiality of personnel.

The processor shall implement appropriate security measures.

The processor shall not engage another processor without authorization.

---

## Article 29 - Processing under the authority of the controller

The processor and any person acting under the authority of the controller shall process data only on instructions from the controller.

---

## Article 30 - Records of processing activities

Controllers shall maintain records of processing activities.

Records shall include:

(a) name and contact details;

(b) purposes of processing;

(c) categories of data;

(d) recipients;

(e) transfers to third countries;

(f) retention periods;

(g) security measures.

---

## Article 31 - Cooperation with the supervisory authority

Controllers and processors shall cooperate with the supervisory authority.

---

## Article 32 - Security of processing

The controller and processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure security appropriate to the risk.

Measures include:

(a) pseudonymisation and encryption;

(b) confidentiality, integrity, availability;

(c) timely restoration;

(d) regular testing.

---

## Article 33 - Notification of a personal data breach

In case of a personal data breach, the controller shall notify the supervisory authority without undue delay and within 72 hours.

---

## Article 34 - Communication to the data subject

When the breach is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, the controller shall communicate the breach to the data subject.

---

## Article 35 - Data protection impact assessment

Where processing is likely to result in a high risk, the controller shall carry out a data protection impact assessment.

This is required for:

(a) systematic and extensive evaluation;

(b) large-scale processing of special categories;

(c) large-scale systematic monitoring of publicly accessible area.

---

## Article 36 - Prior consultation

The controller shall consult the supervisory authority before processing where the impact assessment indicates high risk.

---

## Article 37 - Designation of the data protection officer

The controller shall designate a data protection officer where:

(a) processing is carried out by a public authority;

(b) core activities require regular and systematic monitoring;

(c) processing on large scale of special categories.

---

## Article 38 - Position of the data protection officer

The controller shall support the data protection officer in performing tasks.

The data protection officer shall report directly to the highest management level.

---

## Article 39 - Tasks of the data protection officer

The data protection officer shall:

(a) inform and advise;

(b) monitor compliance;

(c) provide advice on data protection impact assessments;

(d) cooperate with the supervisory authority.
