# GDPR - CHAPTER 5 - TRANSFERS

## Article 44 - General principle for transfers

Personal data may only be transferred to a third country if the controller and processor comply with the conditions laid down in this Chapter.

---

## Article 45 - Transfer on the basis of an adequacy decision

A transfer may be made where the Commission has decided that the third country ensures an adequate level of protection.

---

## Article 46 - Transfer subject to appropriate safeguards

In the absence of an adequacy decision, the controller or processor may transfer personal data if appropriate safeguards are in place:

(a) legally binding and enforceable instruments;

(b) approved binding corporate rules;

(c) standard data protection clauses;

(d) approved codes of conduct;

(e) certification mechanisms.

---

## Article 47 - Binding corporate rules

Groups of undertakings may establish binding corporate rules for transfers.

The rules shall be approved by the supervisory authority.

---

## Article 48 - Transfers to international organizations

Transfers to international organizations are permitted where adequate safeguards are in place.

---

## Article 49 - Derogations for specific situations

Derogations apply for:

(a) explicit consent after being informed of risks;

(b) contract performance;

(c) public interest;

(d) legal claims;

(e) vital interests;

(f) archiving in public interest;

(g) research or statistical purposes.

---

## Article 50 - International cooperation

The Commission and supervisory authorities shall take appropriate steps to develop international mechanisms.
